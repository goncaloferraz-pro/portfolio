# Cosmetics Mobile App — AI-Enhanced Research Design for a Pre-Launch Soft Opening

A cosmetics product discovery app was approaching its public launch and needed to validate its usability and communication with real users before going live. The brief: design and execute a research study with approximately 50 users during a soft opening live session, identify usability and communication issues, and deliver actionable improvement recommendations before the official launch. This case documents the research design phase — a 70-hour, 5-phase methodology with AI-assisted analysis tools mapped to each phase. The research was not executed under this proposal.

---

## 1. Context

The product is a mobile app for cosmetics ingredient discovery and product recommendation — targeting consumers who want to understand what is in their beauty products and make more informed purchasing decisions. The app had been developed and was approaching a soft opening (a live public-facing launch event before the official release), which would serve as the primary usability validation event.

**The research opportunity:** The soft opening was a time-constrained, one-time event with approximately 50 real users interacting with the app live. This is an unusual research context — not a traditional lab session, but a live event with simultaneous users. The research design had to account for real-time data collection across many users at once, using asynchronous capture methods that would not interrupt the live experience.

**The brief:** Design a pre-launch usability study that (1) uses the soft opening as the primary data collection event, (2) integrates AI tools to accelerate analysis of the high-volume, real-time data, and (3) delivers a prioritized recommendations report before the official launch.

**Scope:** 70 hours across 5 phases: planning and preparation, test experience design, live soft opening execution, analysis, and final delivery.

## 3. Method

**Phase 1 — Planning and Preparation (10h).**
Alignment meeting with the client team. In-depth review of the app's critical flows. Definition of measurable validation objectives. Detailed test script planning. Definition of data collection instruments. AI tool used: NLP analysis of product briefings, competitor documentation, and app description to identify key terms, content gaps, and priority areas — grounding the test script in the right vocabulary before writing it.

**Phase 2 — Test Experience Design (15h).**
Development of 3–5 key use case scenarios based on the app's critical flows. Creation of a facilitator script for live session management. Design of visual and interactive support materials. Internal pilot test. AI tool used: Generative AI (LLMs) for scenario drafts and facilitator script variations — accelerating the creative process and ensuring consistency across scenario variants.

**Phase 3 — Soft Opening Execution (20h).**
Live session with approximately 50 users. Facilitation support. Training and alignment of 4–5 internal observers. Real-time user journey monitoring. Data collection via chat think-aloud (adapted for the live format), real-time polling, and post-session questionnaire. AI tool used: Speech-to-text with real-time sentiment analysis — if voice interaction is present, automatic transcription and sentiment detection identify peaks of frustration or confusion in real time, directing observer attention to critical moments.

**Phase 4 — Analysis (15h).**
Organization and standardization of qualitative and quantitative data. Pattern analysis (pain points, opportunities). Data triangulation across sources (chat observations, polls, questionnaires). Insight categorization by urgency, impact, and feasibility. AI tool used: NLP for automated classification and summarization of feedback — extracting recurring topics, quantifying sentiment, identifying patterns across 50 users' chat and questionnaire responses. ML-based prioritization suggests urgency/impact categories, reducing analyst bias and increasing consistency.

**Phase 5 — Final Delivery (10h).**
Visual research report with main learnings. Concrete improvement recommendations. Evidence-based insights with user quotes. Presentation session. AI tool used: Generative AI for draft report sections and data visualization suggestions — accelerating the first draft and enabling more iteration on the final presentation.

## 4. Analysis and Insights

*Note: The research was not executed. The following insights are from the methodology design phase.*

**Design insight 1 — The soft opening format requires adapted data collection methods.**
Traditional usability testing (1 participant × 1 session) cannot run with 50 simultaneous users. The research design adapted three standard methods for this context: think-aloud became a structured chat prompt (what are you thinking right now?); individual observation became a distributed observer team; post-session debriefs became a timed questionnaire while the experience was still fresh. Each adaptation preserved the methodological value while fitting the live event format.

**Design insight 2 — AI analysis is most valuable where volume creates analyst bottlenecks.**
A 50-user session generates large amounts of chat, poll, and questionnaire data that would take days to code manually. AI tools (NLP, sentiment analysis, topic extraction) are applied at exactly the phases where volume creates a bottleneck — not to replace analysis judgment, but to handle the classification and pattern-finding pass that enables the analyst to focus on interpretation and recommendations.

**Design insight 3 — Pre-launch research changes the consequence of every finding.**
Unlike post-launch research, findings from this study could directly prevent a bad experience from reaching the full user base. The urgency/impact/feasibility categorization in phase 4 was designed specifically to distinguish "fix before launch" from "address in the next sprint" — because the pre-launch window is when recommendations have the highest leverage.

## 6. Recommendations and Next Steps

1. **Execute the research before the full public launch** — the soft opening is a one-time, hard-to-replicate opportunity to collect real usability data under real conditions with a controlled audience. Deferring to post-launch removes the ability to act on findings before the full user base encounters the same issues.
2. **Recruit observers who know the product domain** — cosmetics ingredient literacy varies widely. Observers should be able to distinguish "this user is confused because the task is hard" from "this user is confused because they don't know what an ingredient is." Domain knowledge in the observer team affects observation quality.
3. **Define the fix-before-launch criteria explicitly with the development team** — before the analysis phase, agree on what constitutes a "must fix before launch" finding (e.g., task failure rate >40%, any friction in the primary discovery flow). This prevents the recommendations report from generating a week of prioritization debate.

## 7. Reflection

**What worked well:**
- The AI tool integration was designed as a specific fit to each phase's bottleneck — not as a general "AI-assisted workflow" claim. NLP in phase 4 addresses the volume problem. Generative AI in phase 2 addresses the scenario iteration problem. Speech-to-text in phase 3 addresses the real-time monitoring problem. The specificity made the methodology credible and practically usable.
- Designing for the soft opening format's constraints from the start — rather than trying to adapt a standard lab protocol. The chat think-aloud adaptation and distributed observer model are direct responses to the "50 users simultaneously" constraint.

**Challenges and constraints:**
- The research was not executed under this proposal. The methodology design work is the deliverable — the findings that would have come from the research are not available.
- The live event format introduces research validity risks that controlled lab settings avoid: participants may behave differently in a live/public context, the facilitator has less control over task completion, and technical issues during the live event can affect data quality. These risks were acknowledged but not resolved in the design phase.

**What I'd do differently:**
- Include a 15-minute synchronous "hot take" debrief immediately after the live session, before the data has been processed — capturing the observer team's immediate impressions while they are still fresh. These unmediated reactions are often the highest-signal input for understanding which problems were most salient.
